My Story

The Journey So Far

My journey in dance is grounded in rigorous training, research-driven practice, and a sustained engagement with cultural inquiry. I have trained across multiple styles, which provided me with a strong technical foundation and an understanding of discipline and structure. Over time, my interests shifted toward contemporary and modern dance practices, where I found greater space for experimentation, conceptual thinking, and embodied research.

Research plays a central role in my choreographic process. I approach movement creation through observation, analysis, and contextual study drawing from cultural histories, lived experiences, and physical systems. I am particularly interested in how cultural memory, rhythm, and social context influence the way bodies move, relate, and communicate. Before entering the studio, I often engage in research through writing, visual references, music, and conversations, allowing cultural narratives to inform the movement vocabulary that emerges.

As a choreographer, I view dance as both a physical practice and a cultural archive. My work seeks to honor and reinterpret cultural values without reducing them to aesthetic motifs. Instead, I focus on intention, process, and meaning—examining how movement can carry cultural significance while remaining adaptable to contemporary contexts. Concepts such as energy transfer, repetition, and cause-and-effect frequently appear in my work as ways to bridge physical theory with cultural and emotional experience. Navigating spaces shaped by traditional expectations has strengthened my commitment to developing an authentic movement language rooted in research and cultural awareness. By prioritizing contemporary and modern forms, I continue to question hierarchy within dance training and explore choreography as a site for dialogue, reflection, and exchange. Currently, my practice continues to expand through choreography, teaching, and collaborative projects. I see my journey in dance as an evolving research process—one that integrates cultural value, critical thinking, and embodied exploration to create work that is both conceptually grounded and experientially resonant.
