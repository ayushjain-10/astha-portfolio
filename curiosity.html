Areas of Exploration

Where Curiosity Leads Me

Curiosity functions as a foundational methodology within my artistic practice. It drives the way I approach movement, choreography, and interdisciplinary exploration. Rather than working from fixed outcomes, I begin with questions about the body, space, perception, and experience, allowing these questions to guide both research and creation. This openness to inquiry enables my work to remain process driven, adaptive, and responsive. My curiosity extends beyond traditional dance frameworks and into technological and experimental domains. I actively engage with digital tools such as Unity as platforms for creative research rather than solely as technical software. Within these environments, I explore virtual space, interactivity, and spatial composition, examining how choreographic ideas can translate across physical and digital contexts. Technology becomes a site of experimentation that challenges my understanding of embodiment, presence, and audience engagement This research oriented curiosity also shapes how I learn and create. I am drawn to unfamiliar tools and disciplines, using trial, iteration, and failure as integral parts of the creative process. By positioning curiosity as a strength rather than uncertainty, I am able to integrate technology, movement, and conceptual thinking into a cohesive and evolving practice. Ultimately, curiosity allows me to expand the boundaries of dance by treating it as a living system informed by research, experimentation, and interdisciplinary dialogue. It supports my goal of creating work that is embodied, conceptually grounded, and responsive to contemporary artistic and technological contexts.
